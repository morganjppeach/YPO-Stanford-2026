### Areas of expertise
Financial accounting & disclosure; capital markets; investor (esp. retail) behavior; technology/AI & data science in accounting/finance; household finance/consumer credit (e.g., Buy Now Pay Later); market regulation & governance.

### Signature research (with executive relevance)
- **Generative AI in Financial Reporting** (Blankespoor, deHaan, Li, 2024): first rigorous evidence firms already use GAI in filings — up to **~4.5% of new text in 2024** across IPOs, MD&As, risk factors, call remarks, press releases; high-GAI filings have systematically different linguistic properties.
- **Robo-Journalism** (Blankespoor, deHaan, Zhu, RAS 2018): AP's automated earnings articles raised trading volume ~**11%** (retail-driven) and market depth, with **no effect on price discovery**.
- **Weather-induced moods** (deHaan, Madsen, Piotroski, JAR 2017): bad weather makes analysts slower/less likely to respond — even "hard" analysis carries cognitive bias.
- **Strategic disclosure timing** (deHaan, Shevlin, Thornock, JAE 2015): firms bury bad news on Fridays/high-noise days; markets penalize the *signal* of obfuscation.
- **Retail investors & information frictions** (2019/2020): individuals underuse data due to awareness/acquisition costs — AI that lowers processing cost could democratize access.

### Awards

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Use AI to draft SEC filings?"** Peers already do (~4.5% in 2024). Keep a knowledgeable human accountable (the CFO certifies), know detection tools exist, ensure outputs reflect *your* economics.
- **"Will AI replace analysts?"** Supplement, not replace — AI reduces bias/volume work; humans catch novel patterns. Win with AI-augmented analysts.
- **"Protect our stock from AI-generated misinformation?"** Make disclosures machine-readable; monitor AI media; respond fast.
- **"Our auditor uses AI — comforted or concerned?"** Both: more continuous monitoring, but "black box" + training-data limits; ask what it tests and what it can't detect.
- **"One thing tomorrow?"** Map every point AI touches financial reporting and ask: who's accountable, what data trained it, how would we detect an error.
