### Identity & roles
Dan M. Klein — Lecturer in Management, Stanford GSB; faculty in Theater & Performance Studies (TAPS); instructor at the d.school. ~35 years teaching improvisation; **Stanford Teacher of the Year (2009)**. ### Approach (improv → business)
Built a reputation in Stanford's drama department; GSB invited him to adapt improv/performance methods for MBAs and executives. Scientific scaffolding via collaborations with social psychologists.

### Signature frameworks
- **Mining / Crafting / Performing** (the StoryCraft method, co-taught with Michelle Darby).
- **Transformation requirement / "Before and After":** the teller must be changed by the story's climax.
- **The Four Details:** visual, auditory, digital (precise numbers), somatic/kinesthetic — show the physical embodiment of emotion.
- **Delivery craft:** memorize only first & last lines (preserve "life force," ensure a strong landing); avoid filler ("and," "so"); two pauses (between ideas; dramatic); reference the real room ("present orientation").
- **Improv principles for executives:** "be obvious"; "shoot for average and fail cheerfully"; "say the next word, not the good word"; "listening is 80% of the work"; "it's not about you."

### Teaching portfolio
"Acting With Power" (OB 333, with Gruenfeld); TAPS Beginning/Intermediate Improvising; StoryCraft (Stanford Storytelling Project); **"Master Storytelling for Authentic Leadership"** (GSB Exec-Ed online program). Corporate workshops for Google, Meta, Apple, Cisco, Nike, Oracle, Visa, Nestlé, ING.

### Recent work (2024–2026)
Key faculty at **YPO's 2025 Global Business Summit** ("thrive when nothing is certain" via improv — including the "gibberish" exercise). Featured in Stanford GSB's *Think Fast, Talk Smart* (first episode) and "Just Show Up" insight piece; Stanford Storytelling Project feature (2025).

### Distinctive quotes

- "Shoot for average and fail cheerfully."

- "It is not about you." · "Be obvious."

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"I'm a CEO, not a performer — why care?"** Data persuades the rational mind; story moves the emotional mind. You need both.
- **"I'm not a natural storyteller."** These are learnable skills — start before you feel ready.
- **"How do I find stories worth telling?"** Mine for transformation, not achievements — moments of uncertainty, error, surprise.
- **"How much should I rehearse?"** Prepare structure, not script; memorize only first and last lines.
- **"Fear of vulnerability?"** The "Before and After" requires admitting you were different before — that's what builds trust.
- **"Impromptu Q&A/board moments?"** Improv transfers: listen, "say the next word," stay present.
- **"Keep it concise?"** Use the Four Details economically; end on a strong, memorized landing.
