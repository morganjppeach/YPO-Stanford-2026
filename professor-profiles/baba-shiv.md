## 📖 COMPREHENSIVE DOSSIER

### Current roles

- Iowa (1996–2005; "Faculty of the Year" 7 straight years) → Stanford GSB (2005–).

### Signature ideas
- **Two-brain framing:** reflexive (emotional) vs. reflective (rationalizing); the rational brain mostly justifies decisions the emotional brain already made (~90–95%).
- **Price as placebo:** identical wine rated/experienced as better when labeled expensive (medial OFC activation) — "what you expect, you manifest." (2008 Ig Nobel; 2010 AMA O'Dell Award.)
- **Loss aversion compounds** with experience/age → veteran leaders drift risk-averse; counter with diverse teams, innovation mandates, psychological safety.
- **Neurochemistry of innovation:** high serotonin + high dopamine = "calm but energized"; cortisol/stress keeps you on the "beaten path." Type 1 (comfort/risk-averse) vs. Type 2 (exploratory).
- **HRV** as a biomarker of resilience/agility; train recovery from shocks.
- **"Give up the driver's seat"** (TED 2011): too much choice breeds doubt; delegating can be optimal.
- **Resilience:** emotional responses to failure beat purely cognitive ones for subsequent decisions.

### Major work
"Interplay of Affect and Cognition in Consumer Decision Making" (JCR 1999); "Placebo Effects of Marketing Actions" (JMR 2005); "The Role of Emotion in Decision Making" (Scientific American 2006); "Emotions Know Best: The Advantage of Emotional vs. Cognitive Responses to Failure" (2018). Teaches GSBGEN 320 "Neuroscience and the Connection to Sustained Excellence."

### Practical protocol (peak-performance neurochemistry)
Deep/restorative sleep; high-protein breakfast (serotonin/dopamine precursors); cardio + walking meetings; morning light; avoid caffeine when already stressed; no email for the first 30 min; use humor to cut cortisol; schedule creative work in the morning (serotonin dips in the afternoon and after ~50).

### Distinctive quotes
- "It's not making the 'right decision' but making the decision right."
- "What you expect, you manifest."
- "Gut decisions are not flawed."
- "Laughter is one of the most powerful ways to… put the brain into the comfort mindset."

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Trust my gut vs. the board's data?"** Gut often leads; present data as justification, not genesis — instinct distills experience.
- **"Am I just more cautious now, or wiser?"** Likely loss aversion compounding; counter structurally.
- **"My best ideas come when relaxed but I'm slammed."** Protect sleep, eat protein, take walking meetings, skip morning email — innovation is a physiological state.
- **"Over 50 — doomed to risk-aversion?"** Serotonin declines, but safety, humor, morning scheduling, sleep/exercise counteract it.
- **"Build innovation culture in an exhausted team?"** Change neurochemistry first (humor, walking meetings, sleep, psychological safety) before exhorting "think differently."
