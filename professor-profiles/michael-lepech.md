### The seven "Immediate AI Imperatives" (reconstructed — flag as inferred)
1. **Integrate AI into the existing tool stack** (ambient, not episodic).
2. **Restructure teams around AI-augmented roles** (rethink every role description).
3. **Redesign workflows end-to-end** (don't bolt AI onto human-era processes).
4. **Make AI literacy a baseline competency** for everyone.
5. **Build continuous feedback loops** (digital-twin thinking for the org).
6. **Govern AI with clear accountability & guardrails** (data classification, output verification, bias audits).
7. **Transform personal leadership style** (leaders model AI use; question-framer, not just decision-synthesizer).

### Distinctive perspectives
- Sustainability/value is a **design constraint**, not compliance.
- **Uncertainty is manageable, not ignorable.**
- **Technology value is captured, not created** — organizational design fills the gap.

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"We bought AI tools but adoption is weak — why?"** You deployed without restructuring roles (Imp. 2) and redesigning workflows (Imp. 3); usage stays at the margins. Redesign your top 3 workflows with AI as a design constraint.
- **"How do we measure captured (not just spent) value?"** Cycle-time compression, error-rate/quality, and capacity reallocated to higher-value work (the most-missed metric).
- **"Skeptical leadership team?"** Imp. 7 — each leader uses an AI tool daily on a real task for two weeks; experience beats slideware.
- **"Move fast without governance blowups?"** Imp. 6 — make risk visible/governable: data-classification policy, human verification, bias-audit schedule.
- **"Monday morning?"** Pilot a copilot in your most repetitive cognitive workflow; have reports spend 30 min on a real task and report back; use AI yourself on one task.
