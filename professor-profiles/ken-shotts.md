### Signature frameworks
**1) Institutions over intentions.** Drawn from constitutional design: you can't rely on leaders' good intentions; you need checks, incentives, transparency, accountability. A strong mission statement with no enforcement is structurally like a government without checks and balances.

**2) Three-level values architecture.** Individual (values-consistent decisions under pressure) → Organizational (structures that make ethics the default) → Societal (your organization's effect on the broader social fabric).

**3) Values diversity & strategic disagreement.** Articulate your own values; understand others' as a prerequisite to persuasion; design institutional spaces for productive disagreement (procedural agreement even without substantive agreement).

**4) Pandering theory.** From "Leadership and Pandering" (Canes-Wrone, Herron & Shotts, 2001): leaders pander when outcomes are uncertain, observers can't verify private information, and reputational incentives reward appearing aligned over being right. Antidotes are structural (transparency, long-term incentives, independent oversight).

**5) Delegates vs. trustees.** Optimal accountability mode depends on information environment and preference alignment — mapping to "respond to stakeholders" vs. "exercise independent judgment."

### Major publications & cases

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Strong values statement, but we compromise under pressure — how to fix?"** That's the intentions–institutions gap: build values checkpoints into decisions, transparency that makes violations visible/costly, and incentives that reward values-consistent behavior.
- **"My leadership team doesn't share the same values."** Articulate yours; invest in understanding theirs; agree on *process* even when substantive values differ — good institutional design produces good outcomes despite differing preferences.
- **"How do I resist investor/board pressure to compromise?"** Pandering problem — reduce information asymmetry (explain decision rationale), build long-term incentives, establish independent oversight.
- **"How do we build values into culture at scale?"** Organizational level: hire/promote/exit on values-consistent behavior; culture is what you systematically reward and punish, not what you say.
- **"Societal values vs. our business model (e.g., social media & speech)?"** Societal level; see the Meta case — proactive institutional design beats ad-hoc reactive responses.
