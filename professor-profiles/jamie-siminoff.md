## 📖 COMPREHENSIVE DOSSIER

### Snapshot
Founder of Ring (Wi-Fi video doorbell; originally "Doorbot," 2013); acquired by Amazon in 2018 for ~$1B. Left Amazon (2023) → founded Honest Day's Work (acquired by Latch ~$22M; became Latch CEO) → **returned to Amazon in April 2025 as VP of Product** over Ring, Blink, Sidewalk, and Key (also titled CEO of Ring Labs). YPO member.

### Background & path
- Grew up in Chester, NJ, working in his father's forging factory; lifelong tinkerer; cites James Dyson as a model.
- **BS in Entrepreneurship, Babson College (1999)**; honorary Doctor of Laws (2021).
- Six startups before Ring (incl. PhoneTag, first voicemail-to-text, sold). Built the first Wi-Fi doorbell prototype in his garage (couldn't hear the doorbell from the workshop).

### The Shark Tank arc
- 2013 (S5E9): sought $700K for 10% ($7M valuation); "completely broke," spent last ~$20K on the set; all sharks passed; turned down Kevin O'Leary's terms. O'Leary later called it the show's "biggest miss."
- The appearance generated $5M+ in sales — exposure as marketing, not just capital.

### Scaling milestones
| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2014 | Renamed Doorbot → **Ring** (gadget → mission) |
| 2015 | $28M round; Richard Branson stake |
| 2016 | **QVC: $22.6M in one day** |
| 2017 | $109M round; Shaquille O'Neal investor/spokesperson |
| 2018 | **Amazon acquisition (~$1B)** |
Ring is now in 10M+ homes; raised $200M+ (Goldman Sachs, Branson, DFJ Growth).

### Departure & return
CEO of Ring under Amazon (2018–Mar 2023) → "Chief Inventor" → left May 2023. Honest Day's Work (2022–23, residential-services platform) acquired by Latch; Siminoff became Latch CEO. **Returned to Amazon Apr 2025** to lead home-security product, citing AI as "a once-in-a-generation opportunity."

### Expertise
Hardware startups & consumer IoT; product invention (holds core Wi-Fi-doorbell patents); zero-to-10M-homes scaling; branding/mission; defining the "neighborhood safety" category (Neighbors app, law-enforcement partnerships — also a source of privacy debate).

### Signature philosophies & quotes
- **"Too Dumb to Fail"** (forthcoming book): stubborn persistence as structural advantage.
- Buffett: "in the short run the market is a voting machine, but in the long run it is a weighing machine."
- "Big breakouts only happen when people think differently."
- "I was completely broke when I went on the show."
- "A once-in-a-generation opportunity" (on AI).
- Hire fast, for passion over credentials; inventor first, operator second.

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Pivot product or distribution?"** He never pivoted the product (universal problem) but constantly pivoted the channel; let revenue signal which channel to keep.
- **"Public company (Latch) vs. subsidiary (Ring/Amazon)?"** Public = shareholder/stock-price constraints; subsidiary = bureaucracy/loss of autonomy; both informed his return to AI-resourced Amazon.
- **"Inventor vs. CEO identity?"** Steps into operational roles when founder conviction is needed, steps back to product when pros can scale.
- **"Best counter-intuitive decision?"** Turning down O'Leary — the free exposure beat the capital on offer.
- **"AI at Ring now?"** Smarter person/package detection, fewer false alarms, predictive neighborhood safety.
