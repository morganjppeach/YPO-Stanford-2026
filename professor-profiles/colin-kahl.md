### Signature current argument — "The Myth of the AI Race" (Foreign Affairs, Jan 12, 2026)
- **Thesis:** The single-finish-line "race" metaphor is misleading; AI competition fragments across models, compute, standards, and physical-system integration — an **"AI decathlon."**
- **Asymmetric AI bipolarity:** US holds ~70% of global AI compute and a frontier-model edge; China leads **embodied AI** (installed more robots in 2024 than the rest of the world combined). Neither dominates all layers.
- **H200 reversal:** The December decision to permit Nvidia H200 sales to approved Chinese customers risks eroding the US compute edge and could create a "U.S.-enabled Chinese" AI stack abroad.
- **Recommendations:** Slow, scrutinized H200 licensing; enforce lithography controls; support US AI-enabled manufacturing; superpower dialogue on catastrophic (cyber/bio) AI risks.

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Will China win the AI race? Build on Chinese infra?"** No single race; plan for two ecosystems. Western markets → Western stack reduces values/regulatory risk; China/BRI markets may need parallel stacks.
- **"How stable are export controls?"** Politically contingent (H200 shows reversals). Diversify supply chains; treat Commerce licensing as a leading indicator.
- **"What is 'embodied AI' and why care about China's robotics lead?"** AI in physical systems; creates hardware-dependency and data-sovereignty risk for manufacturing/logistics/energy/auto.
- **"US–China conflict & AI escalation?"** Dual-use scrutiny will intensify; scenario-plan a "sudden regulatory lockdown."
- **"Can we count on the rules-based order for data/AI governance?"** No — expect regulatory multiplicity (EU precaution, China state-directed, US innovation-first); design for it.
