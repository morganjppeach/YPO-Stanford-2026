### Signature research
- **"Extraneous factors in judicial decisions"** (Danziger, Levav & Avnaim-Pesso, *PNAS* 2011; ~2,465 cites): Israeli parole grant rates ~65% at session start, falling toward zero before breaks, rebounding after meals — interpreted via mental depletion / status-quo bias. (Also famous for the methodological debate it sparked.)
- **"Order in Product Customization Decisions"** (*JPE* 2010, with Heitmann, Herrmann, Iyengar): decision fatigue from early hard choices → defaulting later; order manipulation shifted car prices by >€1,500.
- **"Virtual communication curbs creative idea generation"** (*Nature* 2022, with Brucks): video calls reduce creative output vs. in-person.
- **"50 Years of Context Effects"** (*JCR* 2024, with Evangelidis): synthesis of how choice context reshapes preference.
- **"Sequential choice in group settings"** (2000, with Ratner & Kahn; ~858 cites): people in groups pick differentiated-but-less-preferred options.
- **"Emotional accounting"** (*JMR* 2009, with McGraw); choice-overload/decision-conflict work (*Management Science* 2022).

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Are my big decisions distorted by fatigue?"** Audit timing — do "default/status-quo" choices cluster late in long meetings? Schedule consequential decisions when fresh; insert breaks.
- **"How do I design choice architecture for my team?"** Sequence matters: lead with easy choices to build engagement; the order effect operates whether or not you design for it.
- **"Is in-person worth it?"** For ideation, yes — virtual measurably curbs creativity; that's why YPO is in person.
- **"How do context effects apply to pricing?"** The alternatives you show reshape preference (compromise/decoy effects) — design your lineup deliberately.
- **"How do I avoid groupthink without losing cohesion?"** Collect independent preferences before group discussion to reduce both conformity and differentiation-for-its-own-sake.
