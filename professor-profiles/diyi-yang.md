## 📖 COMPREHENSIVE DOSSIER

### Current roles
Assistant Professor of CS, Stanford (since 2022); leads the SALT Lab; affiliated with Stanford NLP, HCI, SAIL, and HAI; faculty in Stanford's "Generative AI: Technology, Business, and Society" professional program.

### Education & career
- **BS CS, Shanghai Jiao Tong (2013); MS (2015) & PhD (2019), Carnegie Mellon (LTI)** — advised by Robert Kraut (social computing) and Eduard Hovy (NLP). Postdoc at Google; Assistant Professor at Georgia Tech (2019–22) → Stanford (2022–).
- 34,500+ citations.

### Signature themes
- **Socially aware NLP** (Computational Linguistics, 2025): language is inherently social; model who speaks, to whom, in what context — for persuasion, identity, norms.
- **LLM agents & social simulation** (co-organized 1st LASS workshop): what agents can/can't do in workplaces. **CooperBench**: "Why coding agents cannot be your teammates yet" — they code in isolation but fail at coordination/communication/adaptation.
- **Persuasion & communication:** "Inducing Positive Perspectives with Text Reframing" (ACL 2022 Outstanding Paper) — reframing language for well-being.
- **Privacy & robustness:** "Searching for Privacy Risks in LLM Agents via Simulation" (ICLR 2026); adversarial attacks on vision-language computer agents (ACL 2025).
- **Low-resource & fair NLP:** VALUE/Multi-VALUE (dialects); bias toward AAVE; NormBank (social norms).
- **AI for education/social good:** FairytaleQA; AI companions; parenting media.

### Honors
Sloan Research Fellowship (2024); ONR Young Investigator (2024); NSF CAREER (2022); Microsoft Research Faculty Fellow; Forbes 30 Under 30; IEEE "AI's 10 to Watch"; multiple best-paper awards. NeurIPS 2025 invited speaker.

### Can do now vs. cannot yet (grounded)
- **Can:** reframe/optimize communication; build user models/personalization; power voice assistants; assist research/coding; simulate social behavior (structured).
- **Cannot yet:** function as a true teammate (CooperBench); guarantee privacy in agent architectures (ICLR 2026); reliably resist adversarial manipulation; operate well without social-context modeling; serve sustainable human goals without intentional design.

### Recent focus (2024–2026)
Pivot toward GenAI/LLM agents while staying human-centered; teaches CS 329X "Human-Centered LLMs" and CS 224N. 2026 prediction: the key challenge is "reorienting AI toward sustainable human goals."

### Likely executive Q&A (grounded)
- **"Replace junior devs with coding agents?"** Use them as accelerators for individuals, not teammates — coordination/ambiguity are the gaps (CooperBench).
- **"Is an LLM agent secure enough to deploy?"** Audit the whole system (model + tools + data flows + action space), not just the model (ICLR 2026 method).
- **"Will AI replace customer service?"** Co-pilot, not autonomous — effective comms need social context; otherwise generic/tone-deaf.
- **"Timeline for autonomous process-managing agents?"** Single-task automation is near-term; collaborative, multi-agent process management is years out.
- **"Workforce impact?"** Start from what humans need/want; AI transforms work, sometimes mismatching org structures.
